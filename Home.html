Runyi​ Yao

Welcome!

I am currently a second-year DPhil (PhD) student in Linguistics, Philology and Phonetics at Wolfson College at the University of Oxford. My primary research interests are in the areas of psycholinguistics and experimental semantics & pragmatics. My DPhil project, which is carried out in the Language and Brain Lab at Oxford and supervised by Prof Matt Husband and Dr Daniel Altshuler, is investigating causal inference and expectations in sentence/discourse processing. We are combining the insights of discourse coherence theories, the predictive processing model, and psychological theories of discourse processing to address this question.

More about me

I have a broad interest in human language processing and perception. In addition to my DPhil project, I am also interested in predictive language processing, semantics processing, multimodal linguistic processing and sometimes, first language acquisition. Meanwhile, as a native Chinese speaker, I am also interested in some topics in Chinese linguistics, such as classifiers and temporal expressions in Mandarin.  I am open to discussions and collaborations in all these fields.

Currently, I am serving as the Linguistics PGR student representative for the academic year 2023-2024. If you are a PGR student in the Faculty of Linguistics, Philology, and Phonetics, or a postgraduate student at Wolfson, and if you have any issues or concerns you would like to raise or discuss with the faculty board or graduate study committee,  please feel free to  contact me or leave a message in my pigeonhole at the CLP building.

I was/am also a committee member of  Oxford Postgraduate Linguistics Conference (LingO)  2023 & 2024. LingO 2024 will be held on 19-20 June at  Jesus College , Oxford. Click  here to register for the conference before 5 June!

Before beginning my DPhil study, I earned an MPhil in Linguistics, Philology, and Phonetics at Oxford. Prior to that, I completed a BA in Chinese Language and Literature and a BA in History at Jilin University, China.

My non-linguistic interests include films, traveling and dancing. I started learning Chinese dance when I was three. I gave it up many times but picked it up many-plus-one times, and I really enjoy dancing with friends and performing on the stage, although I am not a talented dancer. So a fun fact about me: I wish I could be a dancer in a parallel world where I am not a psycholinguist!
